Cyberattacks and Cyberterrorism

What is cyberterrorism?

Cyberterrorism is a malicious and deliberate attempt by an individual or organization to breach the information system of another individual or organization (Cisco 2022). In this digital era, cyberattacks prove a most imminent threat and danger to the public. Cyberterrorism occurs when premeditated cyberattacks are used a means to a political agenda. Recently, the effects of cyberattacks have been shown with situations like the cyberattack on the Colonial Pipeline (and other attacks on infrastructure) and concerns of cyberterrorism concerning other national entities (Russia, extreme political parties, etc.). As a result, there has been an increase in media depicting the fear of cyberterrorism (Live Free or Die Hard and other cyberattack movies, novels, and media).

Every aspect of our lives has some sort of technology that makes it possible. On a small scale, a cyberattack on our economy includes identity theft. On a much larger scale, cyberattacks on our economy could cause a stock market crash, resulting in an economic recession. If our internet system is down for a long period, knowledge would not be so widespread. It would become difficult to distribute news and information, effectively sending us back into "the dark ages" (for lack of better words).

Cyberattacks could cause outages in the power grid, effectively disconnecting local communities from the greater society. Transportation would be dangerous, the laws and direction of the road are dependent on technology (stoplights, etc.) The supply chain (food distribution, health care and services, etc.) would be in turmoil, as communication and transportation would be nearly impossible on a large scale; people would become dependent on local resources alone. So much of our daily consumption are results of global imports or interstate commerce. The problem with entering this new age of technology is that it leaves nearly all domains of society susceptible to cyberterrorism. To protect ourselves against cyber terrorism, we must dedicate some resources to cyber-protection as well as round-the-clock service against deterring cyberattacks.
